Project Goal

This information is being collected to help improve video calling software.To do so we need to collect videos ranging from 10-50 seconds of scenarios consisting of specific actions done through a laptop camera with the help of recruited participants.  Each video will contain its own requirements  and participants will have provided their own demographic information.  

Anything below the time requirement for each scenario will not be accepted, nor will videos be accepted where the participant did not follow the instructions.

The goal of this document is to clarify the process in which participants can upload videos and ensure they are of great quality.


For this, the videos must be recorded in landscape or webcam format (RECORDED HORIZONTALLY, NOT VERTICALLY), different formats will not be accepted. They also must be in 1080p your camera and/or webcam has to be able to record at that ability naturally .

Remember that only 1 participation per person, no payment will be made for double participations.If you upload anything that is NOT your own recording and some random video you will be blocked.

Definitions

Scenarios

There are 4 scenarios to be captured for this project. A scenario in the context of this project is just the set of actions you need to do for a given recording. The 4  different scenarios will be discussed in a section below.

Video Call

In a few of these scenarios it will say to act like you are on a Video Call at your computer. This just means that your camera will need to be on and you will need to be viewing the screen as if you were on a call with someone on the other end and act as if you were on a video call. There are some specific actions we’ll go over in a section below.

 Scenarios

In this section we will be listing all the scenarios that need to be captured. As well as their lengths, and best practices. The names/titles are what we call internally but they are not meant to be explicit.

  1. Background Blur: Instruction Set 1
  1. While it may say blur in the name do not worry because we won’t have you blur a thing! Do not blur your background.
  2. Duration:  At least 10 seconds
  3. Set-Up: Face in a direction such that your background is complex/textured. This means the following:
  1. Avoid blank walls.
  2. Avoid bright windows.
  3. Avoid windows with blinds.
  4. Try to have doors, painting, plants, chairs, or other objects in the background view of the camera.
  1. Actions to Capture: Speak and move normally as during a video call. A variety of hand gestures and positions should be captured - as you would on a video call! Act naturally.

  1. Eye Contact Standard: Instruction Set 1
  1. Duration:  At least 10 seconds
  2. Actions to Capture: Imagine you are on a video call with a friend or coworker. In a calm, relaxed state, look at the center of the screen (not your camera) for 10 seconds. Talk or move gently as you would in a call.

  1. Eye Contact Enhanced
  1. Duration:  At least 14 seconds
  2. Set-Up: Have a wikipedia article page loaded on your browser ready to read 
  3. Actions to Capture:
  1. Start by looking directly at the camera for 3 - 4 seconds
  2. Read the Wikipedia article on web browser for at least 10 seconds

  1. Automatic Framing: Instruction Set 1
  1. Duration: At least 30 seconds
  2. Set-Up: Have yourself in a location where you can get up and move away from your chair.
  3. Actions to Capture: In a relaxed, calm manner and while looking at the screen:
  1. speak for 5 seconds from one location
  2. move to a different part of the room, have this take at least 10 seconds
  3. speak for another 5 seconds
  4. When you stand up and get away from the camera, you have to be seen without any obstructions (for example: don’t stand up behind a chair).

(Ensure you are never further than 2m/6ft from camera)

Common Errors

  • Super crucial that the videos are in landscape mode - strictly horizontal! Think more in  the form of a Youtube video instead of a Tiktok video, we do NOT use vertical videos.

  • Lighting - depending on the set you are doing the lighting must be done accordingly!

  • Emphasis on the video feeling natural , we provided some examples to help the recording flow naturally.

  • Make sure when you upload you upload into the right set! If you record the conditions for an Automatic Framing video, upload it to the Automatic Framing one!

  • Distance - Be sure to record your whole face, NOT just your eyes or eyebrows!

  • Camera movement vs Body Movement - If the set you are chosen for requires movement make sure it is your body that moves in the camera, NOT you moving the camera.
  • Be sure to use a camera with 1080p resolution/quality. Blurry videos do NOT work.
